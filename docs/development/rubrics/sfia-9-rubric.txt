SFIA 9 Assessment Rubric

Scoring Guidelines:
- Level 1-2: Foundation competence, works under routine direction with limited autonomy
- Level 3-4: Practitioner, works with some independence, follows established patterns
- Level 5-6: Expert, designs solutions, influences the organisation, takes responsibility for outcomes
- Level 7: Strategic leader, sets direction, shapes organisational capability

Evidence Scoring:
Listen for concrete examples of the following five Generic Attributes:

Autonomy — How independently does the candidate make decisions?
  Look for: self-directed work, decisions made without manager approval,
  ownership of outcomes, setting their own priorities.

Influence — What is their sphere of impact on others?
  Look for: coaching team members, shaping technical direction, engaging
  stakeholders, driving organisational change.

Complexity — What scale and ambiguity of problems do they handle?
  Look for: novel problems, cross-functional challenges, ambiguous requirements,
  high-stakes decisions, systemic thinking.

Business Skills — How business-aware are they?
  Look for: commercial awareness, stakeholder management, risk identification,
  budget ownership, strategy alignment.

Knowledge — What depth and breadth of technical knowledge do they demonstrate?
  Look for: depth in specific domains, breadth across disciplines, knowledge
  sharing, staying current, applying theory to practice.

Mapping Evidence to Levels:
Use the skill definitions provided via RAG context to ask level-appropriate
probing questions. Assign level 1-7 based on the strongest demonstrated
attribute across all five dimensions. A candidate does not need to show all
five at the same level — map to the level that best fits the preponderance
of evidence.

Probing Techniques:
- "Can you tell me about a specific time when..."
- "What was your role in that decision?"
- "Who else was involved, and how did your contribution differ?"
- "What made that problem particularly challenging?"
- "How did you know when you had the right answer?"
